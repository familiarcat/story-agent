## Jonah's business model (extrapolated — Quark)
1. **Distressed acquisition → deep value-add rehab → forced appreciation** (equity spread is the product, not rental yield first).
2. **Local labor-cost arbitrage** — "grunt work super cheap"; margin comes from buying low + cheap local labor vs post-reno comps ($375–475/sf).
3. **Referral vendor network** — trades pre-qualified by referral (quality/price trust), not open bidding.
4. **Phased capital deployment** — draw-based budget tied to milestones; contingency buffered.
5. **Multiple exit strategies** — flip / rent / refinance; ROI modeled per acquisition price.
6. **Economics** — $/sf in vs post-reno $/sf out; equity-% sensitivity to acquisition price is the key lever.
